Patterns of Climate Change Impacts on Precipitation Data

Study Focus

Precipitation pattern and assessment of its impact is crucial for water resource management as well as risk management. Precipitation patterns are often characterized as its seasonality and cyclical features. However, due to climate change, non-cyclic anomalies are becoming more apparent, and it's important to unmask the impact of climate change. This study focused on answering whether there is significant change point observed arount 1960s, the period when the climate change is assumed to have been happened. The precipitation data are extracted from NASA website, using CRU TS 2.0 dataset, which is composed of monthly grids of observed climate with each grid covering the global surface at 0.5° by 0.5°. Locations were chosen and categorized according to their zonal mean precipitation as shown in Figure 1.

Selected Locations

Wet locations: Quito, Ecuador; Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia; Mogadishu, Somalia (around latitude 0)
Moderate locations: New York, USA; Muscat, Omen; Hanoi, Vietnam; Cape Town, South Africa; Cancun, Mexico; Canberra, Australia (around latitude 0 - 65)
Dry locations: Anchorage, USA; Reykjavik, Iceland; Stockholm, Sweden (around latitude 65)

Method

This project operated 2-sided Mann-Kendall(MK) test for a trend detection and Kolmogorov-Smirnov(KS) test and Cramer von Mises(CvM) test for a change point detection. We concluded that there is a trend or a change point when p-value under 0.025.
